Mission Concept

Zone C doesn't need more footage — it needs proof. VARUNA turns "we have drone footage" into "here are 3 confirmed survivors at these coordinates", entirely onboard, in real time.

Core Objective

A VTOL quad-plane surveys 5+ km² autonomously while an onboard AI pipeline converts raw aerial footage directly into actionable rescue intelligence — not a dataset for someone else to process later.

Round 1 → Round 2

Round 1 established the problem framing, UAV justification, and a preliminary VTOL concept. Round 2 delivers a fully dimensioned airframe, justified hardware selection, closed mass/power/endurance calculations, a costed BOM, and constraint-by-constraint compliance proof.

Innovation & Originality

Trusted before it's ever geotagged.

Most reconnaissance drones stop at "here's footage." VARUNA cross-checks every RGB detection against a co-registered thermal signature before it's confirmed — rejecting debris, animals, and reflections onboard, in flight.

Two-sensor voting, onboard

Every RGB-based candidate from YOLOv8 is checked against a thermal signature in the 28–38°C human range via the FLIR Boson 640, before it's ever written to the survivor list.

The vote runs entirely on the Jetson Orin Nano — no ground-station round trip, no manual review queue. Rescue teams get a list they can act on immediately, not one they have to verify first.

UAV Design & Physical Viability

Built for unprepared, debris-strewn terrain.

A twin-boom EPP-foam/carbon-spar airframe, sized with margin at every load path — from hover thrust to hard-field landings.

Airframe

Twin-boom EPP-foam/carbon-spar construction keeps structural mass to 2.05 kg while withstanding repeated hard-field landings on skid gear. Centre of gravity holds within 2% of mean aerodynamic chord via a sliding battery tray — trimmable from vertical hover through 15 m/s fixed-wing cruise.

Propulsion Sizing

Hover: 6.4 kg MTOW → 62.8 N weight → 125.6 N total thrust at 2:1 target → 31.4 N per lift motor, sizing the KV380 + 13" prop. Cruise: 148 W draw at 15 m/s (L/D ≈ 13, η ≈ 0.75) — the basis for the 118-minute endurance figure.

Structural & Flight Simulations

Verified before it's built.

FEA and CFD confirm the highest-load joint and cruise aerodynamics hold well inside standard UAV safety margins.

Zone-Specific Constraint Compliance

Every Zone C requirement, closed.

1Survey at least 5 km²
Autonomous grid-search plan covers 5.0 km² using 50 km of transects at a 150 m swath — validated against 118-minute endurance vs. a 64-minute flight time.
2Fly autonomously
ArduPilot AUTO mode on the Pixhawk 6C executes waypoint navigation, grid-search, obstacle avoidance, and Return-To-Launch with zero manual piloting input.
3Detect 3+ survivors with geotagged coordinates
Onboard YOLOv8 detections are cross-verified against thermal signatures via the FLIR Boson 640, then geotagged using RTK-GNSS position fused with gimbal angle.
4Produce Flood Extent Map + Damage Assessment Report
Survey imagery becomes a georeferenced orthomosaic via SfM; NDWI-informed CNN segmentation produces the Flood Extent Map, and instance segmentation drives a 5-tier Damage Assessment Report.
